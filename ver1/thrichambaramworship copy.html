Trichambaram Sree Krishna Temple

Daily Puja & Darshan Schedules

POOJA TIMING
The temple opens at 5.00 am in the morning and after Uchha pooja the temple will be closed at 12 noon. The temple opens again at 5 pm. The Deepaaraadhana is performed after 6.00 pm and the last pooja, the Athazha pooja takes place at 7.00 pm. Women’s can enter after Athazha pooja.

Important Rituals & Poojas

Moonnambalam

Taliparamba Temple is also connected with two more temples—Thrichambaram Sree Krishna Temple and Kanjirangad Sree Vaidyanatha Temple. Taliparamba, Thrichambaram, and Kanjirangad temples are collectively called Moonnambalams (three temples). Those who visit Taliparamba temple should also visit Thrichambaram and Kanjirangad temples. Visit to these three temples is called Moonnambalam Thozhal.

Though it is not practised as widely as it was in the earlier days, it is still a religious custom among many local Hindu women to visit these prominent temples in Taliparamba when they are pregnant. For getting good children and for normal delivery, pregnant women (before 5 months) usually visit these three temples. It is believed that Shiva at Rajarajeshwara temple assures the child a high status, Sri Krishna of Trichambaram bestows good nature and mental qualities, and the deity Vaidyanatha at Kanjirangad temple grants long life.

Festival Celebrations

The annual temple festival (Utsavam) is a fortnight-long event beginning on Kumbham 22 of Malayalam calendar (which generally falls on 6 March) every year with the Kodiyettam (hoisting of a religious flag) and ends on Meenam 6 (which generally falls on 20 March) with Koodipiriyal (ending of this festival). In between these dates, for 11 days, Thitambu Nriththam (a form of dance with the idols of Krishna and Balarama) is held at Pookoth Nada (1 km from Trichambaram temple

Customs to be Followed

Dress Code & Entry

  • Do not enter the nalambalam wearing or keeping in hand shirt, banyan, pyjama, lungi, bermuda, three fourths etc.
  • Devottees are not allowed to enter the temple wearing chappels.
  • Only Hindus are allowed to enter the temple.

Offerings & Prasadam

  • Offerings to the Lord should be put in the hundika (bhandaram).
  • Devottees are not allowed to enter nalambalam keeping in hand any other temple's prasadam.

Temple Conduct

  • Devottees are not allowed to circummbulate after Athazha pooja.
  • For videography and photography, receipts should be obtained from the counter.
